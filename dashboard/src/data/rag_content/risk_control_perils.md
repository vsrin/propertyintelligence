# Risk Control Recommendations by Peril Type

## Hurricane / Windstorm

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Minimal hurricane exposure, typically inland locations
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Some wind exposure, occasional tropical storm impact
- **7-8 (High)**: Significant hurricane risk, within 50 miles of coast
- **9-10 (Severe)**: Direct coastal exposure, historical major hurricane impacts

- Add hurricane straps at all roof-to-wall connections
- Develop and test hurricane preparedness plan annually
- Pre-position emergency supplies and generator fuel
- Establish contracts with debris removal vendors pre-season
- Install flood barriers for ground-floor openings
- Review business interruption coverage adequacy

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Verify roof attachment meets current building code
- Install storm shutters or plywood pre-cut for all openings
- Trim trees within falling distance of structures
- Secure outdoor equipment and signage
- Review emergency response procedures
- Confirm backup power for critical systems

**Low Risk (1-3)**
- Standard wind mitigation per local code
- Basic emergency preparedness plan
- Annual roof inspection

## Earthquake / Seismic

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Minimal seismic activity, stable geology
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Some seismic risk, fault proximity
- **7-8 (High)**: Active seismic zone, historical significant events
- **9-10 (Severe)**: Major fault zone, high probability of significant event

### Recommendations by Risk Level

**High Risk (7-10)**
- Commission seismic vulnerability assessment by structural engineer
- Retrofit unreinforced masonry (URM) buildings per ASCE 41
- Brace or remove unreinforced parapets and chimneys
- Anchor all non-structural elements (HVAC units, shelving, equipment)
- Install flexible connections on gas lines and utilities
- Secure water heaters with approved strapping
- Bolt building to foundation (cripple wall bracing)
- Install automatic gas shut-off valves
- Develop post-earthquake inspection protocol
- Pre-position structural inspection resources
- Consider base isolation for critical facilities

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Anchor tall furniture and equipment
- Secure suspended ceilings with diagonal bracing
- Install seismic gas shut-off valve
- Brace unreinforced masonry veneers
- Review structural drawings for soft-story conditions
- Train occupants on earthquake response procedures

**Low Risk (1-3)**
- Basic anchoring of heavy equipment
- Standard building code compliance
- Emergency supply kit

### Construction-Specific Concerns
- **Pre-1975 buildings**: Likely pre-date seismic codes, require assessment
- **Soft-story buildings**: Ground floor parking/retail creates weakness
- **Tilt-up concrete**: Wall-roof connections critical failure point
- **URM buildings**: Highest vulnerability, retrofitting essential

## Flood

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Zone X, minimal flood history
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Zone X500, some flood potential
- **7-8 (High)**: Zone A, within 100-year floodplain
- **9-10 (Severe)**: Zone AE/VE, high-frequency flooding expected

### Flood Zone Definitions
- **Zone X**: Minimal flood hazard, outside 500-year floodplain
- **Zone X500**: Moderate risk, 0.2% annual chance (500-year)
- **Zone A**: High risk, 1% annual chance (100-year), no BFE determined
- **Zone AE**: High risk, 1% annual chance, Base Flood Elevation determined
- **Zone VE**: Coastal high hazard, wave action, 1% annual chance

### Recommendations by Risk Level

**High Risk (7-10)**
- Elevate critical equipment above Base Flood Elevation (BFE) + 1 foot
- Install backflow preventers on all floor drains and sewer connections
- Use flood-resistant materials below BFE (concrete, pressure-treated wood)
- Relocate electrical panels, HVAC equipment above BFE
- Install sump pump with battery backup
- Deploy flood barriers (sandbags, flood panels) with 24-hour notice
- Store valuable inventory on upper floors or elevated platforms
- Develop flood response plan with trigger points
- Maintain flood insurance with adequate limits
- Document all contents with photos for claims purposes

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Install sump pump in basement/lowest level
- Elevate major equipment 12+ inches above floor
- Verify floor drain backflow prevention
- Grade exterior to direct water away from foundation
- Clear debris from storm drains regularly
- Review flood insurance options

**Low Risk (1-3)**
- Basic drainage maintenance
- Sump pump recommended for below-grade spaces
- Standard property documentation

## Wildfire

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Urban location, no wildland interface
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Some vegetation exposure, limited interface
- **7-8 (High)**: Wildland-urban interface, significant fuel load
- **9-10 (Severe)**: Direct wildland exposure, historical fire path

### Recommendations by Risk Level

**High Risk (7-10)**
- Create defensible space: Zone 1 (0-30 feet) - lean, clean, green
- Zone 2 (30-100 feet) - reduce fuel continuity, limb trees up 6-10 feet
- Install Class A fire-rated roofing (asphalt, tile, metal)
- Screen all vents with 1/8" metal mesh (ember resistant)
- Replace combustible siding within 10 feet of ground
- Remove combustible materials from under decks
- Install dual-pane or tempered glass windows
- Create gravel or concrete perimeter around structures
- Clear gutters of debris regularly during fire season
- Develop evacuation plan with multiple routes
- Register for emergency alerts (Reverse 911, etc.)
- Maintain vegetation clearance per local fire code
- Install exterior sprinklers for structure protection

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Maintain 30-foot defensible space minimum
- Use fire-resistant landscaping
- Keep roof and gutters clear of debris
- Store firewood 30+ feet from structures
- Screen vents with 1/4" mesh minimum
- Ensure adequate emergency vehicle access

**Low Risk (1-3)**
- Standard landscaping maintenance
- Keep gutters clean
- Store combustibles away from structures

## Tornado / Hail (Convective Storm)

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Outside primary tornado/hail corridors
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Occasional severe storm activity
- **7-8 (High)**: Within tornado alley, frequent hail events
- **9-10 (Severe)**: High-frequency tornado zone, very large hail common

### Recommendations by Risk Level

**High Risk (7-10)**
- Designate and fortify interior safe room (FEMA P-320/P-361)
- Install impact-resistant roofing (Class 4 hail rated)
- Protect HVAC condensers with hail guards
- Install storm shutters or impact-resistant glazing
- Reinforce garage doors (weakest entry point)
- Secure roof with enhanced fastening schedule
- Develop severe weather action plan with clear triggers
- Install NOAA weather radio with alarm function
- Pre-identify shelter locations for all occupants
- Document roof condition with dated photos
- Consider hail-resistant skylights or elimination

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Identify interior shelter areas away from windows
- Install Class 3+ impact-resistant shingles
- Protect rooftop equipment
- Trim dead branches that could become projectiles
- Review severe weather procedures annually
- Verify adequate glass coverage in insurance

**Low Risk (1-3)**
- Basic severe weather awareness
- Standard roof maintenance
- Identify interior shelter location

## Storm Surge

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Inland or elevated location, minimal surge exposure
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Some coastal exposure, moderate elevation
- **7-8 (High)**: Low-lying coastal, within Category 1-2 surge zone
- **9-10 (Severe)**: Direct coastal, within Category 3+ surge zone

### Recommendations by Risk Level

**High Risk (7-10)**
- Elevate building above maximum expected surge level if feasible
- Install breakaway walls on ground floor per coastal building code
- Use marine-grade corrosion-resistant materials at ground level
- Elevate all utilities and mechanical equipment
- Install flood vents to equalize hydrostatic pressure
- Reinforce foundation for wave action and scour
- Develop evacuation plan with 48-72 hour triggers
- Pre-position recovery resources inland
- Consider sacrificial ground-floor design
- Maintain comprehensive insurance including flood and wind

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Elevate critical equipment above expected surge
- Install backflow prevention on all drains
- Use flood-resistant materials at ground level
- Develop surge response procedures
- Monitor hurricane forecasts closely

## Terrorism

### Risk Score Interpretation
- **1-3 (Low)**: Non-iconic, non-critical location
- **4-6 (Moderate)**: Some proximity to high-profile targets
- **7-8 (High)**: Major metropolitan, near government/transit
- **9-10 (Severe)**: Iconic building, critical infrastructure, high visibility

### Recommendations by Risk Level

**High Risk (7-10)**
- Implement vehicle standoff using bollards, planters, barriers
- Install blast-resistant window film or laminated glazing
- Upgrade building access control systems
- Implement visitor management and screening procedures
- Install comprehensive CCTV with retention policy
- Conduct security vulnerability assessment
- Coordinate with local law enforcement on threat intelligence
- Develop active shooter/emergency response procedures
- Train all staff on suspicious package/activity recognition
- Consider progressive collapse analysis for structural design
- Install mail/package screening capability
- Implement cyber security protocols for building systems

**Moderate Risk (4-6)**
- Basic access control and visitor management
- CCTV at entrances and parking areas
- Staff awareness training
- Emergency action plan development
- Coordinate with building security if multi-tenant

**Low Risk (1-3)**
- Standard security practices
- Basic access control
- Emergency contact information posted
